Chili Recipe

This hearty and flavorful chili recipe is simple yet packed with rich, savory flavors. Ground beef (or turkey) is browned and combined with sauteed onions and garlic, then simmered with diced tomatoes, kidney beans, and a blend of spices like chili powder and cumin. The dish comes together quickly with just a few ingredients, making it perfect for a weeknight dinner or casual gathering. It's thickened with a touch of broth or water and allowed to simmer, allowing the flavors to meld and deepen.

The result is a satisfying, comforting bowl of chili that's both versatile and easy to customize. You can adjust the spice level by adding more chili powder or some heat with a dash of hot sauce, and serve it topped with shredded cheese, sour cream, or fresh green onions for extra flavor. Whether enjoyed on its own or paired with cornbread, this chili is a go-to for any chili lover

Ingredient list:

  • 1 pound ground beef (or ground turkey)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (14.5-ounce) can diced tomatoes
  • 1 (15-ounce) can kidney beans, drained
  • 1–2 tablespoons chili powder (to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup water or broth
  • Optional toppings: shredded cheese, sour cream, chopped green onions
  • Optional: pinch of sugar (to balance acidity, if needed)

Recipe steps

  1. Brown the meat: In a large pot over medium heat, cook 1 pound of ground beef (or turkey) until fully browned.
  2. Drain fat: Carefully drain off any excess fat from the pot.
  3. Saute: Add 1 chopped onion and 2 minced garlic cloves to the pot. Cook for 3-5 minutes, until the onion softens
  4. Add main ingredients: Stir in 1 can of diced tomatoes, 1 can of drained kidney beans, 1–2 tablespoons of chili powder, 1 teaspoon cumin, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1 teaspoon black pepper.
  5. Add liquid: Pour in 1 cup of water or broth and mix everything together well.
  6. Bring to a boil: Turn the heat up and bring the mixture to a gentle boil.
  7. Simmer: Reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered for 30–45 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chili thickens.
  8. Taste and adjust: Taste the chili and adjust the seasoning as needed. Add a pinch of sugar if it tastes too acidic.Taste and adjust: Taste the chili and adjust the seasoning as needed. Add a pinch of sugar if it tastes too acidic.
  9. Serve: Ladle into bowls and top with shredded cheese, sour cream, or chopped green onions if desired.
