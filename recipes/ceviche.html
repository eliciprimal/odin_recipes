Ceviche Recipe

This refreshing shrimp and imitation crab ceviche is a vibrant, citrusy dish perfect for warm weather or as a light appetizer. The cooked shrimp and imitation crab are combined with crisp cucumber, juicy tomatoes, and tangy red onion, all marinated in fresh lime and lemon juice. The addition of cilantro and optional jalapenos adds a burst of flavor and a touch of heat, while the creamy avocado provides richness and balance. Served with crispy tortilla chips or tostadas, this ceviche is an easy, crowd-pleasing dish that's both satisfying and light.

With its bright flavors and fresh ingredients, this ceviche is ideal for a summer gathering or as a zesty side to tacos and grilled seafood. It's quick to prepare, can be made ahead, and is easily customizable to suit your preferred spice level or flavor preferences.

Ingredient list:

  • 1/2 pound cooked shrimp, peeled, deveined, and chopped
  • 1/2 pound imitation crab meat, shredded or chopped
  • 1 medium red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
  • 1 cucumber, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2-3 limes, juiced (about 1/4 cup fresh lime juice)
  • 1-2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (optional for extra citrus flavor)
  • 1-2 jalapenos or other chili peppers, finely chopped (optional, for heat)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (optional for richness)
  • 1 avocado, diced (optional, for creaminess)
  • Tortilla chips or crispy tostadas, for serving

Recipe steps:

  1. Prepare the shrimp: Chop the cooked shrimp into bite-sized pieces and place them in a large mixing bowl.
  2. Add the imitation crab: Shred or chop the imitation crab meat and add it to the bowl with the shrimp.
  3. Chop the veggies: Finely chop the red onion, dice the tomatoes, peel, seed, and dice the cucumber, and chop the cilantro. Add all of these to the mixing bowl.
  4. Add the citrus: Squeeze the juice of 2-3 limes (about 1/4 cup) into the bowl, along with 1-2 tablespoons of fresh lemon juice (optional for extra tang).
  5. Add heat (optional): If you like a bit of spice, finely chop 1-2 jalapenos (or another chili pepper) and add them to the mixture.
  6. Season: Add salt and pepper to taste, and drizzle with 1 tablespoon of olive oil (optional for added richness).
  7. Toss to combine: Gently mix everything together until well combined, making sure the shrimp and crab are evenly coated in the citrus juice and seasonings.
  8. Chill: Cover the bowl and refrigerate the ceviche for at least 30 minutes to let the flavors meld.
  9. Serve: Just before serving, gently fold in diced avocado for creaminess (optional). Serve with tortilla chips or crispy tostadas on the side.
